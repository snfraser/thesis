Plan

In this bit describe what the whole project is about and how things will be done -experimental methods
  1. Investigate metrics with which to compare the efficiency and quality of generated schedules.
  2. Design software instrumentation to embed within the current robotic control system to collect data with which to characterize the operating environment.
  3. Using data collected by the embedded software, I will design a simulation framework incorporating knowledge of the operating environment characteristics with which to test any schedulers developed.
  4. Develop a despatch scheduler as a baseline with which to test more advanced schedulers, this will be capable of configuration to use different scheduling policies.
  5. Tune the baseline scheduler using various controllable parameters, typically objective weighting functions and biad function, to investigate the limits of this scheduling paradigm in the telescope scheduling context.
  6. As the basis for the final system, I will develop a look-ahead scheduler incorporating short-term prediction of environmental statistics to allow advanced planning of observations and using feedback of performance to adjust the plan objectives.
  7. Investigate variation of scheduling control parameters and horizon length on quality of schedules generated under varying environmental models to determine how to adapt to these - teh quality of schedules generated will be compared to those geenrated by the baseline system working at its maximum efficiency.
  8. Knowledge gained from this investigation will be incorporated into a final adaptive, look-ahead scheduler.
