Classification of scheduling problems

Some ideas on classification of scheduling problems:-

Integration Depth
How much of the hierarchy of planning, scheduling, execution (and control) is involved and is there feedback and from/to which level(s).
Flexibility
Are the results of the scheduling (i.e. the generated schedule to be followed) fixed or does it contain some flexibility. This may be in the form of temporal or resource assignment flexibility (windows in a fixed execution path) or a graph of multiple execution futures - i.e. contingency/redundancy .
Proactive/reactive
Is the scheduling performed online or offline. ()Better name? - proactive at one end, reactive at other end of a scale)
Method
Constructive or repair based, DDS is sort of in between. Distributed techniques may confound this?.
Horizon
The time period or number of tasks over which the schedule is expected to be followed.
Completeness
The number of tasks from the set of available/assignable tasks to insert into the schedule. We can extract the following progression:-
  1. Despatcher - Scheduler seeks the best single task to perform from the set of assignable tasks at an instant.
  2. Short Horizon - Scheduler selects a sequence of tasks from a subset of the assignable set to perform.
  3. Complete - Scheduler must assign all assignable tasks.

Note: In this scheme DDS is variable depth (typically S with feedback from E), neccessarily fixed, online with horizon 1 task, using a repair? based method.

Q:- What advantages/disadvantages do these measures convey - integrate into paras above?

Notes:- Completness/horizon - how do we determine the length of the horizon or do we select a subset of tasks from pool to execute over a horizon? Several ways:-

  • Use knowledge of the environment dynamics and uncertainty in execution times to choose a hard-coded horizon length.
  • Adaptively select horizon length from measured environment dynamics.
  • Use higher level goal-based planner to select a subset of pool for execution over the next horizon.
  • Combine the last 2.
