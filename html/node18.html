Reactive scheduling

Discuss examples or RS and DS .

Reactive scheduling deals with the repair of executing schedules which have become inconsistant or broken due to changes in the environment. If the goals (objectives) are changed dynamicaly, an executing schedule may break or become sub-optimal, reactive scheduling deals with this situation also. A subclass of reactive scheduling is dynamic scheduling (see 2.8), where the decision of what to schedule next is made at that point in time - there is no look-ahead though the history of the execution to date may be available.

[Jones and Rabelo, 1998] describes 2 classes of reactive system:-

  • reactive repair - the system waits for an event to occur before attempting to recover the system.
  • in pro-active adjustment the system monitors continuously, predicting the future evolution and attempting to plan ahead for contingencies while the plan is executing.
